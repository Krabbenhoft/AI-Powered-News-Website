NPR News Summary - April 4, 2025

Top Stories

  • Trump Tariffs and Economic Impact: Investors, businesses, and consumers are worried about President Trump's tariffs, with analysts saying a near-term deal to end the trade war with China is unlikely. The tariffs are expected to raise grocery prices. The tariffs will also disproportionately hurt the automotive industry in Michigan and have a massive impact on the small African country of Lesotho. Markets are experiencing selloffs.
  • National Security: Air Force Gen. Timothy Haugh was fired as director of the NSA, the latest of several senior officers fired by Trump. The US continues its bombing campaign against Houthi fighters in Yemen, with limited information coming from the Pentagon. President Trump is downplaying the role of a far-right activist in the firing of NSC staff. The Justice Department is being pushed to explain the Alien Enemies Act flights by Judge Boasberg.
  • TikTok Ban Delay: President Trump is again delaying the TikTok ban.
  • Food Recall: Over 200,000 pounds of Egg Beaters and Bob Evans products have been recalled due to potential cleaning solution contamination. Consumers are advised to avoid these products.
  • NEH Staff on Leave: A majority of NEH employees have been placed on immediate leave, following terminations of grants for many humanities councils.
  • Russell Brand Charged: Criminal charges have been filed against Russell Brand regarding allegations of sexual misconduct dating back to 2023.
  • Sean 'Diddy' Combs: The rap mogul faces new sex trafficking charges.

  • Former President Oscar Arias's Visa: Former Costa Rican President Óscar Arias Sánchez fears the U.S. revoked his visa over criticisms of Trump.
