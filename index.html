Recent News Summary - April 6, 2025

Global Conflicts and International Relations

The ongoing war in Ukraine saw intensified conflict as Russian air strikes targeted Kyiv, the Ukrainian capital. This comes as the death toll from a missile strike in Kryvyi Rih the previous Friday continued to rise.

In Yemen, US strikes killed at least two people in a Houthi rebel stronghold. This occurred a day after former President Trump posted a video that is thought to be a signal to the Houthi rebel group, possibly indicating that action was soon to come.

Efforts to mediate peace between Ukraine and Russia continue; however, a shift in public opinion regarding this has been underway.

US Domestic Affairs and Politics

Protests labelled "Hands Off!" occurred across the United States, uniting a range of criticisms against the administration of President Trump. These rallies addressed various issues from proposed child labor law adjustments in Florida, cuts and layoffs in federal departments, and mass deportations. This follows Trump firing the general in charge of the National Security Agency and his deputy.

President Trump's 25% tariffs on foreign cars have gone into effect, leading to immediate economic effects, including layoffs and pauses in car shipments. Jaguar Land Rover Automotive announced it would halt shipments to the U.S. amid the tariff implementation. Efforts are underway by Vietnam to negotiate a reduction in these tariffs. These tariffs are expected to raise consumer prices, although the administration has been slow to release information on what the effects will be. There have been responses from other automotive companies as well. Toyota is holding their prices steady, while Ferrari has raised their prices by as much as 10%.

Funding for a program to upgrade aging low-income housing and improve energy efficiency is being frozen. This action threatens hundreds of projects around the country, especially impacting seniors, particularly those who do not have air conditioning.

A child in West Texas has died from a measles-related illness. This is occurring as the outbreak continues to grow.

Entertainment and Arts

Jean Van Leeuwen, the author of the well-known "Oliver and Amanda Pig" children's book series, has died at 87 years old.

Amadou Bagayoko, the guitarist and half of the Grammy-nominated duo Amadou & Mariam, has died in his hometown of Bamako.

Sports

Alex Ovechkin of the Washington Capitals scored his 895th career goal, surpassing Wayne Gretzky as the NHL's all-time scoring leader. Ovechkin's historic goal came on a power play from the left faceoff circle, as Gretzky watched on. Wayne Gretzky held the record for more than 25 years.

The University of Houston beat Duke 70-67 in the Final Four round of the NCAA Men's Basketball Tournament.

Natural Disasters

A slow-moving and punishing storm system unleashed life-threatening flash floods and powerful tornadoes across parts of the South and Midwest, from Mississippi to Kentucky. The death toll has risen to at least 18.
