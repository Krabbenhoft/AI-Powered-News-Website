May 28, 2025: Market Reacts to Fed Minutes, Nvidia Earnings Impress, and Trade Uncertainties Linger

Today, May 28, 2025, the stock market experienced a downturn driven by a combination of factors including the release of Federal Reserve meeting minutes, earnings reports, and lingering trade concerns. Let's delve into a more detailed account of today's financial news.

Overall Market Performance

The Dow Jones Industrial Average, S&P 500, and Nasdaq Composite all closed lower, cutting short a brief rally seen in the previous session. Investors were closely analyzing the latest Federal Reserve meeting minutes and awaiting earnings reports from major companies, particularly Nvidia. This data will be essential to determine the near term outlook for US equities.

The three major indexes concluded Wednesday's session in the red.

Federal Reserve's Cautious Stance

The Federal Reserve released minutes from its May meeting, which revealed a cautious approach to monetary policy amidst economic uncertainty. Participants indicated that they might face "difficult tradeoffs" if inflation rises, signaling concerns about the potential for persistent inflationary pressures. This stance reflects a broader uncertainty about the economy's trajectory, as officials are carefully evaluating the impact of ongoing trade tensions and tariff policies implemented by the Trump administration. The Fed's current stance is one of 'wait and see', after it had, over the previous months, assumed we were near a recession, followed by overheating risks by mid-May, according to Raymond James.

Nvidia's Earnings: A Mixed Bag

Nvidia, a key player in the artificial intelligence chip market, released its earnings report after the closing bell. While the company beat estimates, it also offered downbeat guidance. Despite strong demand for its graphics processors, particularly for AI applications, Nvidia is facing headwinds from U.S. export restrictions to China. CEO Jensen Huang warned that these export controls threaten to undermine the US's chip lead. There were reports that the Trump administration urged US firms to halt some semiconductor software sales to China, adding pressure on Nvidia. Nvidia has been exploring options to continue doing business in China, underscoring the importance of this market to its overall revenue.

In fact, Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang noted that the company is leaving billions in potential revenue untapped due to limitations on sales to China.

Sector Performance and Key Movers

Several stocks experienced significant movement today. Abercrombie & Fitch saw its stock soaring after reporting better-than-expected first-quarter earnings and revenue. In contrast, Okta, a security company, saw its stock price drop despite beating fiscal first-quarter expectations, as it stood by its guidance citing macroeconomic uncertainty.

Tariff Volatility and International Trade

Trade deal volatility continues to be a prominent theme in the market. President Trump's willingness to use forceful rhetoric in trade negotiations has fueled uncertainty among investors. A trade court blocked some of Trump's tariffs, providing some relief to the markets. However, trade tensions between the U.S. and its major trading partners, including China and the European Union, are still affecting market sentiment.

Furthermore, traders figure that a 20% drop in stocks makes a 'bear market', and we are currently below that threshold.

M&A Activity and Corporate News

In corporate news, Salesforce's stock rose as earnings showed an AI boost. Broadcom, a semiconductor manufacturer, remains an industry leader with strong profitability, benefiting from key secular artificial intelligence drive tailwinds. Bank of America sees 15% upside ahead for Seagate Technology. The bank also raised its price target on the Broadcom.

In more M&A news, Salesforce CEO Marc Benioff said that they will dust off a disciplined M&A playbook relating to their 8 Billion USD acquisition of Informatica

There have also been reports that BlackRock plans to invest in Circle Internet's upcoming IPO.

Looking Ahead

Looking forward, the market is expected to remain volatile through the summer months, with investors closely monitoring developments in trade negotiations, the Fed's monetary policy, and the broader economic outlook. Specifically, market participants are watching the US budget, and the impacts of tariffs. Key economic indicators and corporate earnings reports will continue to drive market sentiment and investment decisions.

UBS sees 'limited' upside for equities in 2025, with rally to resume next year, especially since trade deal volatility will likely be the market theme this year.
