May 2, 2025: Market Rebounds Amid Trade Talk Hopes and Strong Jobs Report

The stock market experienced a significant rally on Friday, May 2, 2025, driven by a confluence of factors, primarily easing concerns over the trade war with China and a surprisingly robust jobs report. The S&P 500 is on track to record its longest winning streak in two decades, effectively erasing the losses incurred following President Trump's "Liberation Day" tariff announcement on April 2, 2025. This announcement, which involved sweeping tariffs, had initially sent shockwaves through the market.

Strategists at BNP Paribas conducted an analysis of stock market crashes over the past century. Their analysis suggests that the recent market activity aligns with previous non-recessionary crashes, which are often characterized as "large and volatile but tend to be relatively brief." This interpretation helps explain the stock market's resilience despite ongoing debates about a potential economic downturn.

Key Market Drivers

  • Easing Trade War Fears: A significant boost to investor sentiment came from signals that China is considering resuming trade talks with the United States. Reports suggest that Beijing is evaluating Washington's recent overtures and is willing to negotiate if the U.S. rolls back reciprocal tariffs. This development eased concerns that tariffs would severely impede economic growth, bolstering market confidence.
  • Strong Jobs Report: Adding to the positive sentiment, the April jobs report revealed a resilient labor market. The U.S. economy added 177,000 nonfarm payrolls, exceeding economists' expectations of 135,000. This strong employment figure alleviated concerns about an economic slowdown stemming from tariff-driven uncertainty. The unemployment rate also remained steady, reinforcing the notion of a healthy labor market. Construction hiring rose in April, adding 11,000 jobs and growing year-over-year.
  • Treasury Sell-Off: As investors became more optimistic about the economic outlook, they started selling off U.S. government debt, which had been a safe haven during the period of heightened uncertainty. This "aggressive Treasury selloff" further illustrated the shift in market sentiment away from a recessionary outlook.

Sector Performance and Key Stock Movements

The technology sector led the market's advance, with Microsoft and Meta showing remarkable gains. The Nasdaq Composite experienced a significant surge on AI trade that led to strong earning reports.

Apple, despite reporting earnings beats, experienced some stock volatility due to concerns about the potential impact of tariffs and the company's focus on AI. However, CEO Tim Cook downplayed any potential tariff impact, but analysts are still uncertain as to whether they believe him or not.

In contrast, Block (formerly Square) experienced a sharp decline in its stock price after cutting its full-year gross profit outlook, citing economic uncertainty. Wells Fargo downgraded the stock, citing monetization red flags and its inability to recommend.

Economic Indicators and Analysis

While the overall picture is positive, some economic indicators warrant caution. Anirban Basu, chief economist at Associated Builders and Contractors, noted that April is likely the last month of economic data largely unaffected by tariffs. He mentioned that the economic outlook has worsened in recent weeks.

The April jobs report provided the first tangible evidence of the trade war's impact on hiring. The data showed that the manufacturing sector is in decline, with manufacturers facing trade war headwinds. The construction industry, despite weak construction spending in March, added 11,000 jobs in April. However, as Anirban Basu noted, this growth might be affected by trade tariffs in coming months.

Global Market Overview

Asian markets responded positively overnight following announcements from China's Commerce Ministry that it was evaluating a reversal of tariffs in negotiations with the U.S. Japan and Australian markets rose following choppy trade on Wall Street.

Other Key Developments

  • Gold Prices Decline: As trade tensions eased, and with solid jobs reports, gold prices headed for their second weekly loss as optimism drew investors back into the stock market.
  • Airline Industry Gains: Delta Air Lines and United Airlines were among companies experiencing gains following the bright U.S. economic data.
