May 2, 2025: Stocks Surge Amid Trade Talk Hopes and Strong Jobs Data, S&P 500 Recovers "Liberation Day" Tariff Losses

The U.S. stock market experienced a broad rally on Friday, May 2, 2025, fueled by optimism surrounding potential U.S.-China trade talks and a surprisingly robust April jobs report. This marks a significant turnaround following a period of volatility induced by President Trump's sweeping tariff announcements earlier in April, which some analysts have dubbed the "Tariff Crash" and "Liberation Day". These recent gains allowed the S&P 500 to wipe out all the losses incurred since then. The gains marked a long period of positive momentum for the S&P, its most positive such momentum for 20 years.

Market Performance and Key Drivers

  • S&P 500: The S&P 500 achieved its longest winning streak in two decades, fully recovering losses suffered following President Trump's tariff pronouncements on April 2nd. The index is currently trading just above its April 2nd level.
  • Dow Jones Industrial Average: The Dow also gained substantial ground with 3M and Caterpillar leading the charge, reflecting positive investor sentiment.
  • Nasdaq: The tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite performed exceptionally well, driven by strong earnings reports from major technology companies.
  • Treasury Selloff: As investor confidence grew, there was an aggressive selloff of U.S. government debt, reversing trades previously based on economic slowdown fears.
  • Labor Market Resilience: April's jobs report revealed that the U.S. economy added 177,000 nonfarm payrolls, exceeding expectations and demonstrating continued strength in the labor market despite tariff-driven uncertainty.
  • China Trade Talk Overtures: A major catalyst for the market's rebound was China's indication that it is assessing ways to allow trade talks with the U.S. to resume, which eased trade concerns.

Trade War Developments

President Trump's "Liberation Day" tariff plan, announced on April 2, sent shockwaves through the market. The plan has caused concerns of economic recession due to tariff-driven uncertainty. Trump's tariff revenue in April topped $17 billion. In 2025, economists note that the impact of tariffs and trade uncertainty has become more pronounced, particularly affecting manufacturing and consumer preferences.

The market initially went into shock after this unveiling, but as of late, Trump has paused tariffs on goods imported from many countries, excluding China. Beijing has hinted it is willing to discuss a trade deal after the US reached out. It's commerce ministry said the "door is open" if the US agrees to pull back on reciprocal tariffs, paving the way to starting formal negotiations. As stated above, the U.S. economy added 177,000 nonfarm payrolls in April, more than the 138,000 expected. The results are thought to have been caused by better than expected numbers, indicating labor resiliency despite a stock market shock in April over tariff uncertainty.

Sector Performance

  • Technology: Microsoft and Meta showed strong results, with Microsoft gaining over 11% for the week. Apple has tariff problems its Big Tech peers do not. Apple is down more than 1% due to tariff concerns, and saw its $100 billion buyback dissapoint some.
  • Finance: Block plunged over 20% after outlook cut, analyst downgrades.
  • Energy: Gold is headed for second weekly loss as trade tensions ease. The precious metal touched an all-time intraday high of about $3,500 last month, only to slip as the trade war started to show signs of de-escalation.
  • Construction: Construction hiring rose in April despite economic uncertainty.

Economic Outlook

Despite the market's positive response, the overall economic outlook remains debated. While the April jobs report suggests that the labor market has remained resiliant, some key economic indicators suggest possible future volatility.

Strategists at BNP Paribas say their analysis points to a non-recessionary scenario for now. Their research into the past 100 years of equity-market crashes has indicated that the recent price action in equities is “consistent with prior non-recessionary crashes.”

Impacts on Consumer Behavior

Airbnb CFO, Ellie Mertz, noted that foreign travelers are increasingly skipping visits to the US, an indication that President Trump's trade war may be impacting consumer preferences.

Company Specific News

  • Block: Shares plummeted after the company cut its full-year gross profit outlook, citing economic uncertainty.
  • Reddit: Showed positive gains, but had concerns regarding Google Search traffic and user growth.
  • Amazon: Amazon is under pressure due to potentially facing factors from tariff and trade policy. However, Amazon beats on Q1 earnings, but light Q2 guidance sends shares sliding.
  • Kohl's: Kohl's board ousts CEO over policy violations.
  • GM: GM cuts profit guidance, warns of $5B impact from tariffs.
  • CVS: CVS posts Q1 earnings beat, raises full-year guidance.
  • McDonalds: McDonald's earnings miss estimates, US sales slump. It also joins Starbucks and Chipotle in traffic drop amid consumer pressure.

Analyst Commentary

  • BNP Paribas strategist Greg Boutle noted that equity crashes have all been unique.
  • Wells Fargo analyst Andrew Bauch downgraded Block's stock due to monetization red flags.
