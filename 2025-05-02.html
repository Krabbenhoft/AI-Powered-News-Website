May 2, 2025: Stocks Surge on Trade Talk Hopes, Recover Tariff Losses, and Jobs Report Strength

The stock market experienced a robust rally on Friday, May 2, 2025, driven by several key factors, including renewed optimism regarding US-China trade negotiations and a surprisingly strong jobs report that indicated resilience in the labor market despite earlier tariff-related concerns.

Market Performance: A Winning Streak

The S&P 500 not only achieved its longest daily winning streak since 2004, marking nine consecutive days of gains, but also fully recovered from the losses incurred following President Trump's sweeping tariff announcement on April 2, 2025, which some have dubbed "Liberation Day". The Dow Jones Industrial Average and the tech-heavy Nasdaq Composite also climbed significantly, reflecting broad market strength. For the week, the Dow gained 3%, and the S&P 500 rose almost 3%.

Trade Talk Optimism: A Thawing in US-China Relations

A primary catalyst for the market surge was the indication from China that it is willing to engage in trade talks with the US. This development signaled a potential de-escalation in the trade war that has loomed over the global economy for an extended period. The Chinese Commerce Ministry stated that its "door is open" if the US agrees to pull back on reciprocal tariffs, setting the stage for formal negotiations. These comments served to alleviate investor anxieties regarding the potential for further economic disruption stemming from tariffs. This is important because the fear of a full-blown trade war has loomed over the market for several months, as did, previously, the trade disputes of 2018 and 2019.

Strong Jobs Report: Labor Market Resilience

Another significant factor bolstering market sentiment was the release of the April jobs report, which revealed unexpected strength in the US labor market. The US economy added 177,000 nonfarm payrolls in April, exceeding the anticipated 138,000. The unemployment rate dropped to a new low for the year. The construction industry also saw gains, adding 11,000 jobs, signaling that the economy is still performing well despite other indications of weakness. The positive jobs data suggested that the labor market remains resilient, even in the face of recent tariff-related uncertainty, with these concerns rising after the Trump administration levied tariffs on trading partners.

Sector Performance and Key Movers

Big Tech companies drove significant gains, continuing a trend started mid-week with earnings reports from companies such as Microsoft and Meta. Microsoft gained more than 11% over the past five days, its best performance since November 2023. However, not all companies performed well. Block stock plunged over 20% after the services and digital payments company cut its full-year gross profit outlook, citing economic uncertainty. This is important because of the relative safety of this company compared to some of its competition.

Airbnb: A Decline in Foreign Travel to the US

Adding a layer of complexity to the economic narrative, Airbnb CFO Ellie Mertz indicated a decline in the popularity of the US as a destination for foreign travelers. This trend, Mertz suggested, could be linked to the ongoing trade war, which may be impacting consumer preferences. While US visits by foreign travelers account for only a small percentage of Airbnb's overall business (2% to 3%), this observation serves as a potential warning sign regarding the broader impact of trade policies on consumer behavior and tourism.

Looking Ahead: The Impact of Tariffs and Trade Negotiations

Looking forward, the trajectory of the stock market and the broader economy will likely hinge on the outcome of US-China trade negotiations and the potential for further tariff adjustments. Anirban Basu, chief economist at Associated Builders and Contractors, cautioned that April's economic data might be the last to remain largely unaffected by tariffs and related uncertainty. The coming months will be critical in determining the long-term economic consequences of current trade policies.

Despite Trump calling for the Federal Reserve to lower rates again after the jobs report, Fed chair Powell has indicated patience, which may affect the market going forward.
