Trade War and Market Volatility: A Deep Dive

April 10, 2025, witnessed significant turbulence in the US stock market, primarily driven by escalating trade tensions, particularly between the United States and China. This summary aims to provide a detailed overview of the key events and their potential impacts.

The Day's Tumultuous Market Activity

The US stock market experienced a sharp downturn on Thursday, April 10, reversing the gains made on Wednesday. Major indices like the Dow Jones Industrial Average, S&P 500, and Nasdaq Composite all took significant hits. This volatility stemmed from investor reactions to the White House's clarification regarding tariffs on Chinese goods and broader concerns about the health of the US and global economies.

  • Dow Jones: Experienced a volatile day, initially pulling back after tumbling as much as 2,100 points midday.
  • S&P 500: Faced significant selling pressure, nearing bear market territory.
  • Nasdaq: Lost a substantial portion of the gains made in the previous session.

President Trump's Trade Policies: A Rollercoaster

President Donald Trump's trade policies continue to be a primary catalyst for market uncertainty. While he temporarily paused some "reciprocal" tariffs for 90 days, the economic damage from previous tariffs is already being felt. Moreover, the trade war with China is intensifying, with goods coming from China now subject to a hefty 145% tariff.

Economists are saying that even with Trump's recent about-face on tariff levels, it does not undo the economic damage.

Key Tariff Measures:

  • China Tariffs: A 125% "reciprocal" tariff announced earlier this week comes on top of the existing 20% tariff, resulting in a total tariff of 145% on Chinese goods entering the US.
  • Universal Tariff: A 10% tariff on most trading partners remains in effect.
  • Existing Tariffs: 25% tariffs on auto imports, steel, aluminum, and some goods from Canada and Mexico are also still in place.

China, in retaliation, has imposed its own 84% tariffs on US imports, further fueling trade tensions. Despite China's willingness to negotiate with the United States, it will not back down if Trump continues to escalate the trade war, as stated by the Chinese Commerce Ministry.

Recession Fears Persist

Despite some positive data, like the cooling of inflation in March, economists are increasingly concerned about a potential recession. Goldman Sachs estimates a 50% chance of recession in the US, while JPMorgan sees a 60% chance, even after Trump's partial tariff detente.

Market Indicators and Investor Sentiment

Several indicators reflect the current market anxiety:

  • Volatility Index (VIX): Surged 40% on Thursday, briefly trading above 50, a level associated with extreme volatility.
  • US Dollar: Weakened broadly this year, signaling investor concern about the health and stability of the US economy.
  • Gold Prices: Hit a fresh record high above $3,170 a troy ounce, reflecting its status as a safe-haven asset during economic uncertainty.
  • Bond Market: a surge in long-term Treasury yields indicated there may be a bond market reversal

Sector-Specific Impacts

Certain sectors are particularly vulnerable to the trade war and tariff policies:

  • Technology: Heavily reliant on global supply chains, particularly those involving China.
  • Energy: Oil prices tumbled due to fears of reduced demand from China, the world's largest crude importer.
  • Construction: Tariffs on steel, aluminum, lumber, and copper are driving up construction costs.
  • Automotive: Auto tariffs, though reciprocal tariffs were pared back, remained in place, placing additional strain on the used car market.

Tesla

Tesla stock price had an interesting week due to market factors. First, Tesla stock price initially dipped on an escalation of Trump's trade war with China. At that time it was made known that Tesla shares manufactured in the United States with tariffs assessed on Chinese imports would see the price rise by as much as $350 in the U.S. Later, as a sign of uncertainty, the US Steel stock dropped as Trump pushed back on a Japanese deal.

Global Reactions

Global markets initially responded positively to Trump's tariff pause. Japan's Nikkei 225 index surged more than 9%, and European stocks also rose sharply. However, the underlying economic uncertainty and the ongoing US-China trade war continue to weigh on global sentiment.

What This Means

The ongoing trade tensions and tariff policies are creating significant volatility and uncertainty in the financial markets. Businesses face rising costs, consumers may experience higher prices, and the risk of a recession remains elevated. Monitoring these developments and understanding their potential impacts is crucial for investors and policymakers alike.

It is known that tariffs, in general, can create a volatile and uncertain economic climate.
