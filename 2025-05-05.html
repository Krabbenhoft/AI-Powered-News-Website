Market Volatility Amid Tariff Threats and Leadership Changes - May 5, 2025

US stock markets experienced a mixed performance on Monday, May 5, 2025, retreating from a recent rally as renewed trade war anxieties surfaced and investors braced for the Federal Reserve's upcoming policy decision. The S&P 500 was on track to end its nine-day winning streak, a testament to the fragility of the current market environment. These factors cast a shadow of uncertainty over the financial landscape, requiring investors to carefully assess incoming data and policy decisions.

Trade War Fears Resurface

President Trump's recent threat of new tariffs has rattled investor confidence, reviving concerns about a potential trade war with China. This development occurred after a period of relative optimism, fueled by signals that Chinese officials were willing to resume trade negotiations. However, Trump's statement over the weekend indicated no imminent plans to speak with President Xi, underscoring the volatile nature of the trade relationship between the two nations. In particular, Trump announced that he will implement 100% tariffs on films made outside of the United States. In essence, the president claims that American film is being devastated, and the tariffs will save it. These trade disputes have long plagued international markets, and the announcement of new tariffs on foreign movies is nothing new.

Federal Reserve Policy in Focus

Wall Street's attention is now turning to the Federal Reserve's upcoming policy meeting, where the central bank is widely expected to maintain current interest rates. The Fed's position remains complex. On one hand, the US economy has seen slow, but steady growth since 2022, but remains under its full potential. Trump is putting pressure on the Fed to cut rates. This creates a very tricky political dynamic. A rate cut would spur economic growth, but may lead to the economy growing too quickly, which can in turn cause inflation. If the Fed does nothing, Trump may get angry.

Corporate News and Developments

Berkshire Hathaway Leadership Transition

Shares of Berkshire Hathaway experienced a slight dip in premarket trading following Warren Buffett's announcement that he will step down as CEO by the end of the year. Buffett has been at the company since the 1970s, and investors were shocked to see him leave. This marks the end of an era. The company's board has tapped Vice Chairman Greg Abel as his successor, a move that has been anticipated since 2021. In the long run, this transition is unlikely to affect Berkshire Hathaway, as Buffett and his Vice Chairmen have managed it very well for 50 years. Still, many investors were shocked by the news.

Skechers Acquisition

Footwear company Skechers is set to be acquired by 3G Capital for $9 billion, a move that sent the company's stock soaring by 25%. The acquisition, priced at $63 per share in cash, represents a 30% premium over Skechers' current market value. Skechers leadership says that the deal will make them the greatest global brand.

Tesla Sales Decline in Spain

Tesla's stock faced downward pressure as data revealed a decline in sales in Spain during April compared to the previous year. While other brands saw an uptick in electric vehicle purchases, Tesla's brand image has been impacted by Elon Musk's involvement with the Trump administration. This also comes as Elon Musk was looking for a replacement CEO.

Oil Prices Tumble

Oil prices experienced a significant drop, reaching a four-year low, as OPEC (Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries) signaled its intention to increase supply. The expectation of boosted production levels has heightened concerns about a supply glut, leading to a decline in crude oil prices. For the past few years, OPEC has kept supply low, but this seems to be changing. Saudi Arabia, the group's leader, is willing to live with lower prices to expand market share. Oil is now at a four-year low due to this announcement. Goldman Sachs has dropped its forecast on high supply.

Services Sector Shows Mixed Signals

Economic data related to the services sector presented a mixed picture of US economic activity. Pricing impacts related to tariffs are now becoming apparent to businesses, resulting in concern. A slowdown in business activity and hiring in the services sector also signaled a concerning trend. This is a worrying backstory, as the service economy is much bigger than the manufacturing economy.
