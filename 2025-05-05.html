May 5, 2025: Market Reacts to Tariff Threats and Buffett's Succession Plan

The U.S. stock market opened lower on Monday, May 5, 2025, driven by renewed fears of a trade war sparked by President Donald Trump's latest threats to impose tariffs. This downturn interrupts a significant upward trend, with the S&P 500 having previously achieved its longest winning streak in two decades. Adding to the market uncertainty is the announcement from Warren Buffett regarding his eventual departure from Berkshire Hathaway.

Trump's Tariff Threats Revive Trade War Fears

President Trump over the weekend renewed his focus on trade deficits, particularly with China, stating he had no plans to speak with Chinese President Xi Jinping this week, despite earlier hints of potential trade negotiations resuming. Trump has also vowed to slap a 100% tariff on movies produced outside the U.S., claiming it will revitalize the American film industry which he stated was a national security threat and being "devastated" by foreign incentives. This protectionist stance has rattled investors and businesses alike, sparking fears of retaliatory measures from other nations and potentially damaging global economic growth.

This threat comes as Trump faces increasing scrutiny over the economic impact of his trade policies. While the President attempts to distance himself from any negative effects, blaming the "bad parts" on the previous Biden administration and claiming the tariffs "are going to make us rich", businesses and consumers are already feeling the pinch. Companies are scrambling to adjust their supply chains and pricing strategies, while consumers are facing the prospect of higher prices and potential shortages, pushing them to buy ahead of the expected tariff increases. This current attempt is similar to earlier in his term, when he placed tariffs on goods from China, sparking retaliatory tariffs from China on US goods, and further escalating the trade war.

Buffett Announces Succession Plan at Berkshire Hathaway

In a separate significant development, Warren Buffett announced at Berkshire Hathaway's annual shareholder meeting that he will step down as CEO by the end of the year. Greg Abel, currently the vice chairman of non-insurance operations, has been designated as his successor. Buffett, now in his mid-90s, has been a fixture in the investment world for over six decades, transforming Berkshire Hathaway from a textile company into a sprawling conglomerate and a highly respected investment vehicle. While Buffett will remain as chairman, Abel will assume the lead role in operational and investment decisions. The succession announcement initially caused a slight dip in Berkshire Hathaway's B-class shares in premarket trading, but stabilized as the markets opened. Buffett tried to reassure shareholders of his long term belief in the company by stating that he would not sell any of his shares, and that he viewed Abel as a better manager of the company than himself.

Economic Data Mixed Ahead of Fed Decision

Adding to the market's uncertainty is the upcoming Federal Reserve policy decision, expected on Wednesday. The Fed is widely anticipated to hold steady with current interest rates. Although Trump has increased pressure on the Fed Chair, Jerome Powell, in recent weeks, analysts believe the Central Bank wants to remain independent.

Economic data has presented a mixed picture, with some indicators suggesting a potential slowdown. Two recent reports on the services sector were mixed. One report stated that respondents were more concerned with actual pricing impacts due to tariffs than the uncertainty surrounding them, and business was being dragged down by federal agency budget cuts. While another stated that business activity and hiring have come closer to stalling amid plunging business confidence.

Other Notable Market Movements

Beyond the overarching themes of tariffs and Buffett's succession, there were several other notable events shaping individual stock movements:

  • Skechers to be Acquired: Footwear company Skechers announced it will be acquired by 3G Capital for $9 billion, sending its stock soaring.
  • Tyson Foods Beats Estimates: Tyson Foods reported quarterly results, beating profit estimates, although sales were flat year over year.
  • Media Stocks Fall: Media stocks, particularly Netflix and Disney, experienced a decline following President Trump's threat of 100% tariffs on foreign-shot movies.

The market is in a state of flux as it grapples with both global economic uncertainty and the leadership transition of one of the most influential companies in history. Investors will be closely watching the Federal Reserve's decision and any further developments in the trade war.
