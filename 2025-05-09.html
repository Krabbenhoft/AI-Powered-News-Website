May 9, 2025: Market Volatility Amidst Trade Tensions and Economic Data

The stock market experienced a choppy trading day on Friday, May 9, 2025, as investors remained cautious ahead of crucial trade talks between the United States and China scheduled for the weekend. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent is slated to meet with his Chinese counterpart in Switzerland to discuss trade and economic matters. These talks are occurring after the announcement of a preliminary trade agreement between the U.S. and the United Kingdom, the first such deal under President Trump's "reciprocal" tariff policy initiated in early April. The U.K. deal, which establishes a 10% tariff rate as a global baseline, has provided some insight into the potential structure of future trade agreements. However, Goldman Sachs cautions that while de-escalation may be occurring with some countries, other trading partners may still face renewed tariff threats.

President Trump added further uncertainty by stating on Truth Social that an "80% Tariff on China seems right." While this figure represents a de-escalation from the current 145% tariff, it is substantially higher than earlier expectations, which suggested rates could be lowered below 60% this week. It remains unclear whether this rate is intended to be a long-term or temporary measure during negotiations. Investors are reacting to the ebbs and flows of news, creating volatility and uncertainty in the markets.

Meanwhile, Federal Reserve Vice Chair Michael Barr warned that increased tariffs could lead to higher prices and lower economic growth. He expressed concerns about disruptions to global supply chains and persistent upward pressure on inflation, as well as the potential for higher unemployment as the economy slows. His comments echo concerns expressed by other market participants who expect the tariffs to hamper companies' abilities to accurately forecast future performance.

Stock Market Performance and Key Movers
Despite the overall market uncertainty, energy stocks performed well as oil and natural gas prices rose. The energy sector was the top-performing sector in midday trading.
Several companies experienced significant price movements based on their earnings reports and outlooks:

  • Lyft: Shares jumped more than 20% after the company increased its buyback plan and reported better-than-expected gross bookings.
  • Tapestry: Parent company of fashion brands Coach and Kate Spade, was upgraded to "overweight" by Morgan Stanley, with a price target of $90, citing its tariff resilience and brand momentum.
  • Affirm: Shares declined after the company projected weaker-than-expected revenue guidance for the upcoming quarter, though Susquehanna International Group upgraded the stock to a positive rating.
  • Expedia: Shares slid due to lower demand for U.S. travel, leading to weaker-than-expected revenue. Expedia is also lowering full-year outlook amid weak demand in the U.S.
  • Monster Beverage: Rose to a fresh all-time high, becoming the top-performing stock among current S&P 500 members.
  • Insulet: Stock is Soaring to Lead S&P 500 Gainers after it reported stronger-than-expected quarterly results and lifted its full-year revenue outlook.

Economic Data and Analysis
Bank of America reported weak spending trends in April, with total household credit and debit card spending rising only 1% year-over-year. Spending was particularly soft in categories like airlines, clothing, and home improvement. However, some front-loading was observed in online electronics ahead of potential tariffs. Additionally, Wells Fargo noted that the small number of companies pulling guidance this earnings season is a positive sign, although those companies include participants who expect tariffs to hamper companies when forecasting future performance.

Barclays suggests investors should continue to "don't fight Trump" mantra, as the expectation for a de-escalation of trade tensions is already priced into the market. However, due to the uncertainty around the final steady-state tariffs, further meaningful valuation upside may be limited. India has reportedly offered to slash its tariff gap with the U.S. in an effort to secure a trade deal.

Overall, the market is navigating a complex environment characterized by trade negotiations, tariff uncertainties, and evolving economic data, resulting in volatility and a cautious investor sentiment.
