May 9, 2025: US-China Trade Talks Loom, Market Reacts to Trade Developments and Earnings Reports

The financial markets are bracing for crucial trade talks between the United States and China this weekend in Switzerland, led by Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and U.S. Trade Representative Jamieson Greer on the American side. This follows a preliminary trade agreement reached between the U.S. and the United Kingdom, the first such deal under President Trump's "reciprocal" tariff policy announced in early April. The Dow Jones Industrial Average experienced a drop of over 100 points on Friday as investors awaited these talks, indicating some uncertainty.

President Trump has signaled a potential de-escalation with China, suggesting an "80% Tariff on China seems right" in a Truth Social post. This is a significant reduction from the current 145% tariff but still higher than anticipated by many, as some expected rates could fall below 60% this week. It remains unclear whether this is a long-term or temporary measure during negotiations. The president also reiterated that China should open its markets to the U.S.

Federal Reserve Governor Michael Barr has cautioned that tariffs could lead to higher inflation and potentially slower economic growth, a sentiment echoed by economists who fear disruption to global supply chains. Despite these concerns, Barclays suggests investors continue to "don't fight Trump" as the president aims to secure more trade deals.

Market Performance and Key Stock Movements

  • The S&P 500 closed with little change on Friday, reflecting investor caution. Energy stocks outperformed the market due to rising oil and natural gas prices.
  • Tapestry (Coach, Kate Spade) received an upgrade from Morgan Stanley to overweight, citing tariff resilience and brand momentum. The price target was increased to $90, indicating a 16% upside. Tapestry management also confirmed that tariffs will have an "immaterial impact" on the company's 2025 outlook.
  • Monster Beverage reached an all-time high, marking it as a top-performing stock within the S&P 500.
  • Lyft's shares jumped over 20% after increasing its buyback plan and reporting better-than-expected gross bookings. CEO David Risher conveyed optimism about consumer spending despite broader economic concerns.
  • Affirm Holdings Inc received an upgrade from Susquehanna, but its stock dropped 13% due to soft revenue estimates.
  • Wells Fargo noted a "positive" surprise in the earnings season, as only a small number of companies have pulled earnings guidance, potentially boosting investor sentiment. However, several companies expect that tariffs would hamper their company's performance, or have already done so.

Economic Indicators and Analysis

  • Bank of America reported weak spending trends in April, with total household credit and debit card spending rising only 1% year-over-year.
  • Goldman Sachs cautioned that while the U.S. has secured a trade agreement with the U.K., and relations with China appear to be improving, other countries might face renewed threats of country-specific tariff rates.
  • Reuters reported that India has offered to reduce its tariff gap with the U.S. to under 4% in exchange for an exemption from President Trump's tariffs.
  • China's exports increased in April despite U.S. tariffs, while imports experienced a slower decline due to increased stimulus efforts by Beijing.
