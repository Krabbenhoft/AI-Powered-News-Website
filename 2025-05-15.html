May 15, 2025: Market Fluctuations Amid Tariff Concerns and AI Developments

The U.S. stock market experienced a mixed trading day on Thursday, May 15, 2025, as investors grappled with a confluence of factors ranging from persistent inflation worries to developments in artificial intelligence. The Dow Jones Industrial Average saw gains, while the Nasdaq Composite snapped a six-day winning streak.

Inflation and Tariff Impacts

A significant driver of market sentiment was Walmart's announcement that it would be raising prices due to the impact of tariffs. This acknowledgment from America's largest retailer underscores the real-world consequences of the trade policies implemented under the Trump administration, which returned to power earlier in the year. These tariffs, designed to protect domestic industries and pressure trading partners, have had the unintended consequence of increasing costs for businesses and consumers.

Background on Tariffs: Tariffs are taxes imposed on imported goods. Historically, tariffs have been used for two primary purposes: to raise revenue for the government and to protect domestic industries from foreign competition. While tariffs can shield domestic producers from cheaper imports, they also lead to higher prices for consumers and can provoke retaliatory tariffs from other countries, creating trade wars. President Trump's return to the White House in 2025 marked a resurgence of these protectionist policies, undoing some of the de-escalation that had occurred in trade relations in the intervening years.

Walmart CEO Doug McMillon emphasized that the company would strive to keep prices low but admitted that the magnitude of the tariffs, even at reduced levels after recent deals, was too great to absorb completely. He indicated that consumers would begin to see these price increases later in May and more significantly in June. This development confirms the concerns of many economists who have warned that tariffs could lead to inflation, offsetting any potential benefits from other areas of the economy.

Despite Walmart's announcement, wholesale inflation data released on Thursday showed an unexpected slowdown last month. This divergence between corporate forecasts and economic data has left investors uncertain about the true state of the economy. The Federal Reserve, led by Chair Jerome Powell, continues to monitor incoming data closely, with Powell cautioning about the possibility of more frequent "supply shocks" and volatile inflation. He reiterated that the Fed is prepared to adjust its monetary policy as needed to maintain price stability and full employment.

Solita Marcelli, chief investment officer Americas for UBS Global Wealth Management, pointed out that while recent trade agreements have been cheered by investors, the US effective tariff rate remains significantly higher than it was at the start of the year. This lingering uncertainty could continue to trigger market volatility as businesses and consumers adjust to the new trade landscape.

Developments in Artificial Intelligence

Another significant factor influencing market movements on Thursday was news related to the artificial intelligence sector. Meta Platforms, formerly Facebook, experienced a slide in its stock price after The Wall Street Journal reported that the company was delaying the rollout of a flagship AI model. According to the report, engineers were struggling to significantly improve the capabilities of its "Behemoth" large-language model, raising questions about whether the enhancements were substantial enough to warrant a public release. This delay underscores the challenges and complexities involved in developing advanced AI systems and has led to staff questions about the company's direction and progress.

Amazon also faced headwinds as the company announced additional job cuts in its devices and services division. The layoffs, which included 100 positions in the division responsible for Alexa voice assistant, come as part of a broader trend of cost-cutting measures within the tech industry. Since the start of 2022, Amazon has reportedly cut approximately 27,000 employees. These job cuts, coupled with Trump's back-and-forth trade policies and greater scrutiny of Big Tech's AI spending, have put pressure on the "Magnificent Seven" stocks, including Amazon.

Other Market Movers

Several other companies and sectors also experienced significant movements on Thursday:

  • Deere & Company (Deere): Deere's stock hit an all-time high after the company reported better-than-expected profits, despite a 22% decline compared to the same period last year. This positive performance signaled to investors that the agriculture sector may have bottomed out after a period of decline.
  • UnitedHealth Group (UnitedHealth): UnitedHealth stock plummeted, reaching a five-year low, after reports surfaced of a possible Department of Justice (DOJ) criminal investigation into potential Medicare fraud. The company has disputed the allegations.
  • Foot Locker (Foot Locker) and Dick's Sporting Goods: Shares of Foot Locker surged following confirmation of an acquisition offer from Dick's Sporting Goods.
  • Oil Prices: Oil prices tumbled after President Trump hinted at a potential nuclear deal with Iran, which could lead to an increase in global oil supply.

Analyst Commentary

Amid the market volatility, analysts offered various perspectives. Jamie Dimon, CEO of JPMorgan Chase, cautioned about the possibility of a recession, stating, "Hopefully, we'll avoid it, but I wouldn't take it off the table at this point." Joseph Brusuelas, chief economist at RSM, noted the divergence between the bond market and equity market, suggesting that fixed income investors are seeing a more concerning outlook than what the stock market is implying.

Conclusion

Overall, Thursday's market activity reflects a complex interplay of economic factors, geopolitical considerations, and industry-specific developments. Investors remain cautious as they navigate through inflation concerns, trade policy uncertainties, and the evolving landscape of artificial intelligence. The coming weeks will be crucial in providing further clarity on the direction of the economy and the stock market.
