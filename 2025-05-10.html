US Stocks Dip Amid US-China Trade Talk Uncertainty and UK Trade Deal

US stock markets experienced a volatile week, ultimately closing lower on Friday, May 9, 2025, as investors braced themselves for critical trade negotiations between the United States and China. These talks, scheduled to take place in Geneva over the weekend, hold significant weight in shaping the future of global trade relations, particularly amidst ongoing tariff disputes. The Dow Jones Industrial Average, S&P 500, and Nasdaq Composite all reflected this market apprehension.

Background: The US-China Trade War

To understand the current market climate, it's crucial to recall the recent history of US-China trade relations. Tensions have been high for years, characterized by escalating tariffs imposed by both nations on a wide range of goods. In early 2023, President Donald Trump initiated a series of tariffs targeting Chinese imports, citing unfair trade practices, intellectual property theft, and the significant trade deficit between the two countries. China responded with retaliatory tariffs on US goods, particularly agricultural products. This tit-for-tat escalation has negatively impacted various sectors of the global economy, creating uncertainty for businesses and consumers.

The impact of the trade war has been varied, but significant. The US implemented tariffs on Chinese imports that started at 10% and rose to 25% across $250 billion worth of imports. Now, in 2025, President Trump is considering a decrease from 145% to 80%. American businesses that rely on Chinese imports have faced increased costs, sometimes passing those costs on to consumers through higher prices. Furthermore, American agricultural exporters have struggled as Chinese demand for US products has declined. The effects haven't been isolated to just these two countries; global supply chains have been disrupted, and concerns about global economic growth have grown.

Key Developments on May 9, 2025

Here's a summary of the important events impacting the market:

  • US-China Trade Talks Loom: The main driver of market volatility was the anticipation of high-stakes trade talks between US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Chinese officials in Geneva. Investors remained wary about the potential outcome and the long road ahead for a resolution.
  • Trump's Tariff Proposal: President Trump added to the market uncertainty by suggesting, via a social media post, the possibility of cutting US tariffs on Chinese imports to 80%. White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt clarified that the 80% figure was just a number President Trump “threw out there,” and no final tariff rate would be set before this weekend's talks. The current tariffs on imports from China are at 145%
  • US-UK Trade Deal: The announcement of a trade deal between the US and the United Kingdom on Thursday provided a brief boost in market sentiment. There are promises of more trade deals to come.
  • Treasury Secretary Meetings: Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent will be meeting with Chinese officials this weekend to talk about trade.
  • Lyft: A major investor in Lyft will be exiting the ride-hailing company and withdraw its board nominees after the company increased its stock buyback program to $750 million from $500 million.

Sector Highlights

  • Tesla: Tesla stock had a good day as it surged more than 4%, becoming the top-performing "Magnificent Seven" stock. Tesla is also set to close out the week with the biggest gains
  • Pinterest: Pinterest stock is soaring. Ad spending is remaining strong during this time of tariffs.
  • Boeing: Boeing is the first winner in the new era of piecemeal trade deals.

Market Outlook

The market's direction in the coming days and weeks heavily hinges on the outcome of the US-China trade talks. While President Trump's hint at a tariff cut offered a glimmer of hope, significant uncertainties remain. If the talks yield substantial progress toward a comprehensive agreement, it could trigger a significant market rally. However, a failure to reach a consensus could lead to renewed market jitters and further economic strain. Investors will be closely monitoring developments in Geneva for any indications of the future trajectory of this critical trade relationship.
