Market Rally Fueled by Easing US-China Trade Tensions and Stable Fed Chair

The stock market experienced a significant rally on Wednesday, April 23, 2025, primarily driven by two key factors: a softening stance from President Donald Trump regarding trade relations with China and reassurance that Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell would not be imminently removed from his position.

Easing US-China Trade Tensions

President Trump indicated a willingness to de-escalate trade tensions with China, a departure from the more confrontational rhetoric observed in recent weeks. This shift was evidenced by his statement that current tariffs on Chinese imports, which stood at a substantial 145%, would be reduced "substantially," although he clarified they would not be eliminated entirely. This announcement provided immediate relief to markets that had been rattled by the ongoing trade dispute. It is important to understand the background: The US-China trade relationship has been increasingly contentious over the past few years, marked by escalating tariffs and retaliatory measures. These tariffs, essentially taxes on imported goods, have significantly impacted businesses and consumers in both countries, leading to market volatility and uncertainty. The initial imposition of tariffs was predicated on addressing trade imbalances and alleged unfair trade practices. A reduction in these tariffs signals a potential easing of this economic pressure.

Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent further amplified this sentiment, stating that both countries had the potential to reach a "big deal" on trade, suggesting a collaborative approach to rebalancing the trade relationship. He also added, in a speech at the IIF Global Outlook Forum in Washington, D.C., "If they want to rebalance, let's do it together." This echoed earlier positive sentiments on U.S. trade relations with India.

The Wall Street Journal reported, citing White House sources, that the administration was considering reducing tariffs to between 50% and 65%. While a White House official later clarified to CNBC that any such move would require a bilateral commitment from China to lower its own trade barriers, the report nonetheless contributed to the overall positive market sentiment. Copper futures also surged to levels not seen since April 3rd due to optimism about a resolution to the trade impasse.

Reassurance on Federal Reserve Leadership

Adding to the positive market response, President Trump stated he has "no intention" of removing Jerome Powell as Federal Reserve Chair. This statement directly addressed investor concerns about the central bank's independence. In recent weeks, President Trump has been increasingly critical of Powell's monetary policies, particularly regarding interest rates. The role of the Federal Reserve, a central bank, is to manage the nation's money supply and credit conditions to promote economic stability and growth. The Fed achieves this through setting interest rates, influencing inflation, and regulating banks. Any perceived interference in the Fed's independence raises concerns among investors, as it could undermine the Fed's ability to effectively manage the economy. Trump’s previous comments calling Powell a "major loser" and saying his "termination cannot come fast enough" had fueled uncertainty and market volatility. The President’s shift, therefore, brought much-needed stability.

Other Key Economic Developments

  • Manufacturing and Services PMI: Purchase manager surveys from S&P Global painted a mixed picture. Manufacturing activity saw a more-than-expected rise in April, while the services sector experienced a slowdown. Both sectors reported inflationary pressures stemming from tariffs, although some domestic manufacturing companies cited stronger sales due to tariffs. The composite PMI output index, a measure of activity in both the manufacturing and services sectors, fell to a 16-month low in April.
  • US Economic Output: US economic output hit a 16 month low in April.
  • New Home Sales: Housing activity for new residential construction climbed in March, with sales of new single family homes rising to a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 724,000. Inventory also ticked up, with 503,000 homes for sale at the end of the month, the highest level since 2007.
  • "Magnificent Seven" Stocks: The tech giants collectively known as the "Magnificent Seven" led the market rally, with companies like Amazon showing significant gains.
  • Tesla: Tesla's stock experienced an 8% rally after CEO Elon Musk announced he would dedicate more time to the company and reduce his involvement with DOGE (presumably a cryptocurrency-related project). Tesla climbed as Musk said time spent on DOGE will drop "significantly". This news came after a challenging quarter for Tesla. Tesla reported adjusted earnings of 27 cents per share, beating estimates but on revenue that was shy of expectations.
  • Boeing: Boeing shares rose following a first-quarter report that showed narrowing losses compared to the previous year. The company reported a net loss of $31 million, an improvement from the $355 million loss in the same period last year, and they said they were planning to ask the FAA to approve increased production of 737 Max jets.
  • AT&T: AT&T's stock rose after reaffirming its full-year earnings guidance.
  • Enphase Energy: Enphase Energy stock declined sharply, following the firm’s earnings falling short of estimates and the firm providing a weak outlook, citing tariff pressures.
  • Vertiv: The stock rose after the company reported strong earnings and raised its guidance.
  • Bitcoin ETFs: Bitcoin ETFs saw their biggest day since January 17, as they saw a large spike in inflows with interest in the cryptocurrency’s potential as a hedge against the diving dollar.
