Market Reacts to Shifting Trade Policies and Fed Stance

US stocks experienced a volatile day on Wednesday, April 23, 2025, marked by a significant rally followed by a partial retracement as investors reacted to comments from President Trump regarding trade relations with China and his views on Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell.

Trump's Comments on Trade and the Fed

President Trump stated he has "no intention" of firing Fed Chair Jerome Powell, a marked departure from his previous criticism. This statement aimed to soothe Wall Street's concerns about the central bank's independence and the potential for political interference in monetary policy. It's important to remember that the Federal Reserve was established as an independent entity to make decisions based on economic data rather than political pressure. This independence is seen as crucial for maintaining stable monetary policy and investor confidence.

In addition, Trump hinted at a de-escalation in the trade war with China, suggesting that tariffs on Chinese imports would be "come down substantially." These comments were interpreted as a move to alleviate trade tensions and prevent further economic slowing. However, the promise of tariff reductions was not concrete, and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent later clarified that no unilateral offer had been made to China, contributing to the market's pullback. The Trump administration's trade policies have been a source of uncertainty, with businesses and investors closely watching for any changes in direction.

Market Performance

The initial market reaction was overwhelmingly positive, with major stock indexes surging to significant gains. The Nasdaq, which is heavily influenced by technology stocks that are particularly sensitive to trade issues, at one point rose over 4%. The Dow Jones Industrial Average added over 1,100 points in early trading. However, as the day progressed and details remained scarce regarding actual tariff cuts, the market's gains began to diminish, with the Dow eventually closing up 418 points. This demonstrates the sensitivity of the market to policy pronouncements and the importance of tangible actions to sustain investor confidence. The "Magnificent Seven" tech stocks, including Amazon and Nvidia, initially led the rally, reacting positively to the prospect of easing trade tensions. Nvidia's stock performance is particularly notable, as chipmakers are heavily impacted by tariffs.

Economic Indicators and Concerns

Despite the market's initial rally, underlying economic concerns remain. The Federal Reserve's Beige Book revealed that firms are planning to pass price increases on to consumers due to tariffs, potentially impacting consumer spending. Moreover, Goldman Sachs lowered its first-quarter GDP growth expectations due to weaker-than-expected activity in the services sector and soft housing construction. The housing sector, which is sensitive to interest rate changes, has struggled due to approaching 7% mortgage rates. The ISM Composite PMI output index hit a 16-month low in April, indicating a marked slowing of business activity. These indicators suggest that President Trump's tariffs are already impacting economic activity and consumer prices, even if the immediate market reaction on Wednesday was positive.

Safe Haven Assets

The "sell America" trade, a trend where investors had been dumping US stocks and traditional safe havens, partially reversed on Wednesday. The 10-year Treasury yield edged closer to the psychologically important 100 level. Gold prices, which had recently surged as investors sought non-dollar-denominated safe haven assets, retreated, signaling a decrease in investor anxiety. The dollar has been particularly weak and this may signal a slight rebound due to the perceived decrease in uncertainty.

Company News

Several companies were in the news due to their earnings reports and other announcements. Tesla's stock rallied after Elon Musk stated his intention to focus more on Tesla and less on government affairs. However, Tesla's first-quarter results came in below Wall Street forecasts, leaving analysts split on the stock's future. Boeing's stock also popped 5% as it continues its turnaround efforts amid production challenges and trade war issues. Intel shares surged as the chipmaker reportedly plans to cut 20% of its workforce. Chipotle reported a first same-store sales decline since the pandemic, citing diners pulling back on spending.

Analyst Perspectives

Renaissance Macro head of economics, Neil Dutta, suggested that Trump is starting to "feel the market," implying the president is paying more attention to market reactions when formulating policy. Research shows the S&P 500 has responded more favorably to comments from Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent compared to other Trump administration officials. This may suggest the market views Bessent as a more reliable source of information or someone advocating for a more moderate approach.
